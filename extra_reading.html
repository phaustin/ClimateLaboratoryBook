Additional resources¶

  • Books

    • Dennis Hartmann: Global physical climatology

    • Roland Stull: Boundary Layer Meteorology

    • Roland Stull: Practical meteorology

    • Roland Stull: Thermodynamic diagrams (also see Practical Meteorology, Chapter 3, Thermodynamics)

  • Journal articles

    • First demonstration of water vapor feedback: Manbe and Weatherald, 1967

      • most influential paper?

      • JAS pdf

    • CGILS cloud feedback project

      • Initial single column intercomparison paper (2013)

      • Tan et al. with slab ocean (2016)

    • New book: Clouds and climate (2020), by Siebesma, Bony, Jakob and Stevens

      • Chapter 6: Parameterizing clouds, in Clouds and Climate

      • Chapter 7: Evaluating clouds, in Clouds and Climate

      • Chapter 8: Tropical and subtropical clouds systems Clouds and Climate

    • Raditive convective equilibrium

      • Hartmann and Larson, 2009, FAT hypothesis

      • FAT or FiTT, Seeley et al., 2019

    • Climate sensitivity

      • Roe, 2009 on Feedback

      • Zelinka, Klein, and Hartmann,2012 – radiative kernels

      • Sherwood et al., 2019 – climate sensitivity review

      • feedback slides

    • Ocean/atmosphere heat transport

      • Trenberth and Caron 2001, Meridional heat transport

    • Milankovic and Ice Ages

      • Cvijanovic et al. 2020, One hundred years of Milankovic cycles

    • Climate change and Precipitation

      • Allen and Ingram 2002, Constraints on future changes in climate and the hydrologic cycle

      • Held and Soden 2006, Robust responses of the Hydrological Cycle to Global warming

      • Allan and Soden 2008, Atmospheric warming and precipitation extremes

    • Model hierachies, overtuning and the pitfalls of complex models

      • Held 2005, The gap between simulation and understanding in climate modelling

      • Hourdin et al. 2017, The art and science of climate model tuning

      • Emanuel 2020, The relevance of theory for contemporary research in atmospheres, oceans and climate

      • ISCA model hierachy A framework for a comprenhensive model hierachy available on github.

    • Atmospheric Dynamics

      • Vallis et al. 2014: Response of the large-scale structure of the atmosphere to global warming A thorough review.

      • Isaac Held’s blog. This is no longer being updated but has some good summaries and perspectives on relatively recent issues in dynamics and climate change.

    • Machine Learning and Climate Modelling

      • O’Gorman and Dwyer 2018: Using Machine Learning to Parameterize Moist Convection, Climate, Climate Change and Extremes

      • McGovern et al. 2019: Understanding the Physical Implications of Machine Learning

    • Ocean and coupled Atmosphere-Ocean dynamics

      • Wunsch and Ferrari 2018: 100 Years of the Ocean General Circulation. A nice review of the history of the physical oceanography as well as an overview of the circulation itself.
